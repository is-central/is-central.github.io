Kazdel Kasino 2 is a team-based tournament with teams of 3 players. Players earn money by completing objectives during their runs, then convert those dollars into placement points using a summon simulator at the end. The top 8 teams proceed to Round 2; the final placement is also leaderboard-based.

## Format

- Team of 3 players
- Player 3: Difficulty 18
- Players 1 and 2 choose difficulties so that the sum across all three is 48
- After the last player finishes their run, then immediately use the summon simulator to earn placement points
- Leaderboard: Top 8 proceed to Round 2; final placement determined by Round 2 leaderboard

## Rules

### Fated Encounter
Each team cannot select the following relics more than once per team:
- "Talons of Hatred"
- "Lamp of Wishes"

### Wiš'adel
Wiš'adel cannot be picked unless it's a temporary recruit after a combat or Silver Seal.

### Balance Limit Withdrawal
- Players 1 and 2 share 100 ingots; Player 3 has 50 initial ingots to withdraw.
- Every ingot withdrawn past the limit deducts the player \$500 per ingot.

### Run Preparation
- Only the following floor 3 bonus buffs are allowed: Kettle, 2 Hope, 2 Plans.
- Players can finish the first combat but cannot select any relics from the first combat.
- Players must select an encounter as their 2nd node on the floor.
- The 2nd node must be "Fated encounter" and players must stop at this screen.
- If a player does not want a Fated Encounter relic, they must stop after the first combat. Rerolling the 2nd node is allowed.

### Reset
- All players start with 0 available resets.
- Emulator crashes: each player allowed 1 restart per tournament (subject to organizer review).

### Miscellaneous
- Players are allowed to change squads after rerolling
- Players are allowed to openly communicate at all times during their runs.
- The relic “Masterless Memories” is allowed to be selected in the run
- Players may only use the account they used to register with.
- All bonuses and penalties will be calculated at the end of the run.

## Tournament Dynamic
The tournament consists of all players completing a variety of tasks from which they gain dollars, while players 1 and 2 also earn yen.
Player 3 can spend yen in the yen shop to buy bonuses to facilitate their run.
After all runs are completed, the dollars accumulated are used in a gacha simulator to determine the final score.

### Dollars Currency
Starting and throughout the run, dollars are used to buy their starting squad and operators according to their cost.
Throughout the run, players can get more dollars to either spend into more operators or save for the gacha simulator.

- Dollars are spent in: Operators, Starting squads, Final score
- Dollars are obtained by completing: Bias bonuses, General stages, Conditional stages, Achievements, Final collectibles count

### Final Scoring
- After the 3rd player finishes their run, the Team can now spend all the accumulated Dollars on a Summon Simulator.
- A player from the team must stream the following website <a href="https://lungmendragons.com/gachasim" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Lungmen Dragons gacha simulator</a>
- The team can select any banner from the simulator and can change the banner at any time during the session.
- If reset history is clicked, the pulling will continue from that moment onwards. The VOD will be reviewed to determine the score before the reset history was clicked.
- The summon simulator follows the same rules and rates as the in-game summoning system such as pity.
- Teams will receive score based on the rarity of the operators recruited:
  - For Every 6 star operator summoned = 360 pts
  - For Every 5 star operator summoned = 120 pts
  - For Every 3 and 4 star operator summoned = 10pts

## FAQ

**Q**: If Players 1 and 2 both draft the same bias bonus operator, how are the penalty dollars calculated?\
**A**: The base cost of a bias bonus operator is \$25 and is only \$0 when the corresponding bias challenge is completed.

Consider the following scenarios with Młynar being the bias bonus operator:
1. Player 1 completes the Młynar bonus challenge while player 2 drafts Młynar but does not complete the challenge; the incurred cost on player 2 is \$$25 \cdot 3$ = \$75
2. Player 1 does not complete the Młynar bonus challenge while player 2 completes the challenge; the incurred cost on player 1 is \$25
3. Player 1 and 2 both fail the challenge, the incurred cost is \$25 + \$75 = \$100
4. Player 1 and 2 both complete the challenge, the incurred cost is \$0 for both players. However the bonus can only be claimed once (\$$150 \cdot 1$)

**Q**: How does the operator restriction penalty removal work in the Yen shop?\
**A**: The operator restriction penalty removal reward only applies to Player 3, effectively setting the cost of the operator back to its base cost. Players 1 and 2 are not affected by this effect.

**Q**: What is a triple ending?\
**A**: A triple ending is an Integrated Strategies run where three ending boss have been defeated. Ending bosses include Floor 5,6,7 Dreadful foe stages. Clearing only Victory Rewind does not count as defeating an ending boss.

**Q**: What is the "Across 3 Players, select 3 different dradful foes to load the same 3 operators on a boat,..." team achievement? Any examples of what counts/does not count?\
**A**: Consider the following scenarios:
1. Player 1 loads Deepcolor, May and Beanstalk on a boat on Court Visit. Player 2 and 3 also load the same operators on Dharma Sermon and Fate's Finale respectively. The achievement is considered completed.
2. Player 1 loads Deepcolor, May and Beanstalk on a boat on Court Visit and Dharma Sermon. Player 3 also load the same operators on Fate's Finale. The achievement is considered completed.
3. Player 1 loads Deepcolor, May and Beanstalk on a boat on Court Visit and Dharma Sermon. Player 2 and 3 also load the same operators on Dharma Sermon but not Fate's Finale respectively. The achievement is considered incomplete.
4. Player 1 loads Deepcolor, May and Beanstalk on a boat on Court Visit. Player 2 and 3 also load the same operators on Dharma Sermon and Fate's Finale respectively. However, the boat does not reach the blue box in Fate's Finale. The achievement is considered incomplete.

**Q**: Does drafting initial 6★ operators during initial recruitment consume dollars in the starting allowance?\
**A**: No, the choice of your initial operator draft does not impact your ability to choose which starting squad, only your starting allowance. The remainder of your starting allowance is converted into dollars.

**Q**: When are dollar bonuses/penalties calculated?\
**A**: Dollar bonuses/penalties are calculated at the end of the player's individual run.

**Q:** Are Amy list operators increasing in price as all other 6★ operators?\
**A:** No, 5★ Amy list operators are still at \$35 no matter how many players in your team picks them
